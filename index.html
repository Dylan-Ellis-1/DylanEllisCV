International project with Penn State University

Jan 2022 - Apr 2022

The project set out to create an autonomous hydroponic farming system that can be implemented in urban areas. The project was divided into 3 sub-projects, the hydroponic system, the car to transport the arm around the hydroponic system, and the robotic arm. My team oversaw designing and building the robotic arm that would remove containers from the system once the plants are ready and then insert new containers into the system.

For the arm, we made use of a 3D printer to print the components and the different parts of the arm. We bought 3 stepper motors and 2 rack and pinion systems to handle the movement and the rotation of the arm. To control the arm and the motion we made use of a Raspberry Pi. On the Raspberry Pi, we used python for the coding and we were able to get the stepper motors to rotate and turn the arm.
